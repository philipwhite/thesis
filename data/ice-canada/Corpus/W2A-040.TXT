<ICE-CAN:W2A-040#18:1> <h> SUMMARY </h>

<p> <ICE-CAN:W2A-040#19:1> Once flexibility is introduced into the arm of the robot, severe

problems in the accuracy and stability are likely to occur which make control

a critical issue.
<ICE-CAN:W2A-040#20:1> These problems can successfully be eliminated only if the nonlinear

dynamics associated with the flexible—arm is properly accounted for. </p>

<p> <ICE-CAN:W2A-040#21:1> In this paper we study the behaviour of a two degree of freedom

high speed robot with a flexible—arm, having quadratic nonlinearities with

natural frequencies defined as <O> mathematical symbol </O> <sb> 1 </sb> and

<O> mathematical symbol </O> <sb> 2 </sb> , at <O> mathematical equation </O>

internal resonance.
<ICE-CAN:W2A-040#22:1> We perform numerical simulations as well as analytical investigations

on a simplified mathematical model of the system, subjected to periodic

excitation.
<ICE-CAN:W2A-040#23:1> The two variable expansion perturbation method is used to show the

existence of jump phenomena and ` saturation' when both forced resonance and

internal resonance occur.
<ICE-CAN:W2A-040#24:1> Numerical studies indicate the existence of chaotic solutions in the

resonance regions.
<ICE-CAN:W2A-040#25:1> The routes to chaos contain subharmonic bifurcations. </p>
